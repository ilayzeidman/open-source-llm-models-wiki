# Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 (CC-BY-NC-4.0)

source_url: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/legalcode.en
fetched: 2026-05-07

## Definition of "NonCommercial"

> "NonCommercial means not primarily intended for or directed towards commercial advantage or monetary compensation."

File-sharing exchanges of CC-BY-NC material for other CC works are explicitly OK as long as no money changes hands.

## What CC-BY-NC-4.0 forbids

- Using the material in or for products/services that are "primarily intended for commercial advantage or monetary compensation."
- Sub-licensing under terms incompatible with NC.

## What it requires

- **Attribution**: name the licensor, link to the license, indicate any changes.
- Cannot imply endorsement.

## Ambiguities (well-known, repeatedly criticized)

- "Commercial" is undefined as a bright line — internal R&D at a for-profit company is a contested gray zone. Creative Commons themselves describe NC interpretation as fact-specific.
- Wikipedia and other major open-content platforms reject NC works.
